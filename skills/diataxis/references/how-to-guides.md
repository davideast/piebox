# How-to Guides (Task-Oriented)

A how-to guide is an answer to a specific question for an already-competent user. They are at work, they are stuck, and they need to get unstuck.

## Key Principles & The "Tutorial vs. How-To" Tension

- **The user is competent:** Assume they understand the basic concepts. They just don't know the specific steps for this specific task.
- **The goal is practical:** The user has a real-world problem (e.g., "How to migrate a database").
- **Do not teach:** Do not explain how the tool works. Get straight to the steps.
- **Flexibility:** Unlike a tutorial, a how-to guide must allow for the user's real-world context. Use phrases like, "Depending on your setup..."

## Writing Rules

- **Titles:** Must be goal-oriented, usually starting with "How to..." or a gerund ("Configuring...").
- **Language:** Conditional imperatives. "If you need to restart the server, run `restart`."
- **Anti-patterns:** Do not start with a long introduction. Do not promise they will "learn" something.
